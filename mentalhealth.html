College Mental Health

Mental health is an often overlooked part of our overall health. It includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being, and it can be affected by a variety of factors throughout our lives such as life experiences and biological factors. For college students specifically, mental illness can greatly impact a person’s health and well-being. It can present numerous challenges in an already pressure-filled environment and is often stigmatized as well.

For college students, there are numerous resources available related to mental health both on and off campus. By being aware of and taking advantage of these resources, we can better understand and recognize our needs as well as how to be mindful of our mental health overall.

Now, more than ever, it is crucial to be aware of the importance in taking care of our mental health, reaching out when we need it, and fighting the stigma against taking care of ourselves mentally as well as physically. By talking about mental health and the resources available, we can start to normalize taking care of this aspect of our health just as we would with any other aspect.

Mental illness can affect college students in a variety of ways. Here are just some of the issues that college students may face as a result of mental illness.

Lower energy levels

Reduced ability to concentrate

Poorer mental ability

Reduced ability retain information

Poorer academic performance

Reduced motivation

Issues with sleeping

Feeling overwhelmed

Boynton’s Mental Health Clinic — Boynton offers a variety of mental health services. This includes services for individual therapy, couples therapy, group therapy, crisis consultation, medication assessment and management, social work help, services for eating disorders, ADHD and ADD treatment, alcohol and chemical health services, and emotional support animals. Boynton has clinic locations on the East Bank of campus as well as the St. Paul campus.

Student Counseling Services — Student Counseling Services offers a few services related to mental health. They help with mental health and life concerns by offering personal counseling for mental health issues, relationship concerns, and stress-related concerns. They also offer crisis consultation both over the phone as well as in person. Student Counseling Services is located both in Appleby Hall on East Bank as well as in Coffey Hall in St. Paul.

Disability Resource Center — Students with diagnosed mental health conditions may be entitled to accommodations through the Disability Resource Center. The resource center works with students to implement reasonable accommodations for students with various disability conditions, including mental illnesses, to ensure equal opportunities to learn and work. The Disability Resource Center is located in the McNamara Alumni Center.

Fairview Riverside Behavioral Emergency Center — The Fairview Riverside Behavioral Emergency Center provides emergency assessment, referrals, and care to those in crises related to mental health. The emergency center offers a variety of programs for those dealing with mental health issues including inpatient care, partial hospitalization, day treatment, and outpatient programs. They are able to provide assistance with medication management as well as therapy, and are located in Minneapolis.

Hennepin County Acute Psychiatric Services — Hennepin County Medical Center’s psychiatric department is another resource which provides emergency services for mental health 24/7. They offer crisis counseling, assessment, and referral; they also provide both inpatient and outpatient treatment options. Hennepin County Acute Psychiatric Services center is located in Minneapolis.

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline — The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline provides free, confidential support for those in suicidal crisis. This is a national suicide prevention and intervention resource that is available 24/7 at 1-800-273-TALK (8255).

Along with resources for mental health, here are some mental health related initiatives as well. These initiatives present a way to get involved, to bring awareness to mental health, and to fight the stigma against it through organizations and social media campaigns.

StigmaFree — This is a campaign run by the National Alliance on Mental Health. As the name implies, this campaign is an effort to end the stigma attached to mental illness. The campaign focuses on providing ways to avoid and address stigma for those both with and without mental health conditions.

Make It OK — Make It OK is a campaign to encourage open conversations and education on the topic of mental health. They provide information on mental illness, stigma, and how to approach conversations about mental health with the hope of spreading the word and stopping the silence surrounding mental health and how we discuss it.

#RealConvo — This is a social media campaign created by the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention and Instagram. Its aim is to bring more public awareness to mental health and people’s experiences with it, and to inspire people to find supportive communities.

IDONTMIND — IDONTMIND is a mental health awareness campaign and brand aimed at fighting the stigma against mental illness. This is an official program of Mental Health America, it and encourages people to normalize conversations about their mental health and raise money for mental health organizations across the U.S.
